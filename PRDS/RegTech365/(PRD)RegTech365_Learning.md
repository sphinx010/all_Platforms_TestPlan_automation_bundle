## Overview

We are building RegTech365 Learning, an intelligent compliance learning and training platform designed to support organizations and individuals in achieving regulatory awareness, audit readiness, and professional certification. Unlike traditional LMS solutions, the platform is deeply embedded within the RegTech365 ecosystem, ensuring training is aligned with compliance obligations, audit requirements, and organizational trust goals.

The platform delivers structured, modular, and trackable compliance education, supporting both awareness-level training (short microlearning courses for staff) and professional certification training (deep-dive courses for compliance officers, auditors, and implementation teams).

It addresses the complexity of regulatory standards (ISO, NDPR, GDPR, AML, etc.) by providing guided roadmaps, progress tracking, and automated notifications that keep learners and organizations engaged and compliant.

### Key Points:

* Secure user onboarding with role-based access (individuals, admins, organizations).

* Awareness & professional tracks: microlearning for all staff, advanced certification for specialists.

* Compliance learning roadmap aligned with standards (ISO 27001, ISO 22301, GDPR, AML, etc.).

* Quiz engine & assessments with auto-grading and reporting.

* Certificates of completion for professional courses, automatically generated and watermarked.

* Progress tracking dashboards at both individual and organizational levels.

* Notifications & nudges to drive course completion and maintain compliance momentum.

## **Problem**

Organizations face increasing challenges in ensuring that their workforce is adequately trained and prepared for regulatory compliance. Traditional training methods—such as long seminars, static PDFs, or manual workshops—are costly, time-consuming, and often ineffective in driving long-term engagement and knowledge retention.

Compliance teams and HR managers frequently struggle with:

* Tracking completion of mandatory courses across large or distributed teams.

* Assigning training that aligns with specific compliance frameworks (ISO 27001, NDPR, GDPR, AML, etc.).

* Measuring effectiveness of training through assessments, reminders, and completion rates.

* Maintaining accountability without a centralized dashboard to monitor staff progress.

The problem is compounded by the fact that many employees view compliance training as a box-ticking exercise, leading to low motivation, incomplete courses, and poor readiness when audits occur. Without reminders, nudges, or progress tracking, organizations often discover too late that key staff have not completed critical training, exposing the business to penalties and reputational risks.

Most existing LMS solutions are not compliance-focused. They lack structured roadmaps tied to standards, automated notifications to keep learners on track, or the ability to connect learning outcomes directly to audit requirements. This results in:

* Training gaps that leave organizations unprepared for audits.

* Wasted investment in courses that are not aligned to compliance needs.

* Lack of verifiable evidence of training completion during regulatory reviews.

Ultimately, the absence of a purpose-built compliance learning platform leads to missed training obligations, audit failures, and erosion of trust with regulators and stakeholders. For compliance to serve as a true enabler of growth and trust, training must be structured, measurable, and seamlessly integrated into compliance management workflows.

Objectives

The primary objectives of RegTech365 Learning are centered around simplifying compliance training, improving learner engagement, and enabling organizations to demonstrate training compliance as part of audit readiness. Success for the Academy will be measured by its ability to deliver structured learning paths, provide accountability through tracking and nudging, and integrate training outcomes into broader compliance requirements.

1. Simplify Compliance Training & Access

   * Goal: To reduce training complexity by providing structured, categorized courses (awareness, security, data protection, etc.) with both free and paid options.

   * Success Indicator: At least 80% of users can easily browse, enroll, and complete training modules within their first week on the platform.

2. Improve Completion Through Engagement & Nudging

   * Goal: To increase training completion rates by embedding nudges, reminders, and deadlines assigned by organization admins.

   * Success Indicator: 60–70% of learners complete assigned courses within the set deadline, compared to \<40% with traditional manual training.

3. Support Continuous Audit Readiness via Training Evidence

   * Goal: To provide organizations with verifiable training records and progress dashboards that align with compliance frameworks (ISO 27001, GDPR, NDPR, etc.).

   * Success Indicator: At least 75% of organizations report improved readiness to demonstrate staff training compliance during audits.

4. Enable Flexible Learning for Different Audiences

   * Goal: To make training accessible to both internal staff (assigned courses only) and external learners (full catalog), ensuring inclusivity.

   * Success Indicator: Organizations can assign courses to 100% of staff, while at least 50% of individual learners outside organizations adopt additional professional training.

5. Position Learning as a Compliance Training Hub

   * Goal: To establish the Learning Academy as the go-to compliance training platform within the RegTech365 ecosystem.

   * Success Indicator: Within two years, the Academy is adopted by at least 3 major regulated industries (e.g., finance, consulting, technology) as part of their compliance culture.

   ## Constraints

Content Development

* Challenge: The success of the Learning depends on high-quality, up-to-date compliance training content (awareness, professional, and certification courses). Outdated or generic courses may fail to meet regulatory needs.

* Impact: Requires continuous content development, expert partnerships, and updates aligned to evolving standards.

User Engagement

* Challenge: Many staff view compliance training as a “box-ticking exercise,” leading to low motivation and incomplete courses. Without proper nudging or gamified elements, engagement may remain low.

* Impact: Investment in user experience features (e.g., reminders, nudges, dashboards, quizzes, microlearning) is critical to achieving adoption.

Resource Limitations

* Challenge: Building both the learning engine and integrated payment/cart features requires significant engineering and financial investment.

* Impact: A phased rollout may be needed, prioritizing free awareness training, dashboards, and admin features in MVP, with payment integrations and advanced features added later.

Market Adoption & Trust

* Challenge: Organizations may be hesitant to adopt a new LMS if it does not clearly align with audit requirements or if evidence reporting is weak.

* Impact: The Academy must position itself not only as a training platform but also as a compliance-enabler that provides verifiable, audit-ready training evidence.

## Use Cases

RegTech365 Learning is designed to serve a variety of user scenarios, tailored to the specific needs of its diverse personas. Below are four scenarios illustrating how different users might engage with the platform.

Scenario 1: Course Assignment and Staff Nudging

* Persona: Organization Admin

* Context: Ada, a compliance manager, wants to assign ISO 27001 awareness training to her IT team and ensure they complete it within two weeks.

* Steps:

  1. Ada logs into the Learning and selects “Awareness Training.”

  2. She chooses the ISO 27001 awareness module and assigns it to 20 staff members.

  3. She sets a deadline of 14 days.

  4. The platform automatically sends each staff member a notification with details.

  5. Ada uses the “Nudge” button to remind staff who are behind schedule.

* Outcome: Staff complete training on time, and Ada’s dashboard shows progress for each learner.

---

Scenario 2: Individual Learner Browsing and Enrollment

* Persona: Independent Learner

* Context: Michael, an IT professional, wants to improve his compliance knowledge. He visits the platform, browses all categories, and enrolls in a cybersecurity awareness course.

* Steps:

  1. Michael signs up as an individual.

  2. He browses course categories: Awareness Training, Cybersecurity, Data Protection, Business Continuity.

  3. He adds a free awareness course to his shelf.

  4. He also adds a professional paid course on “Secure Coding” to his cart and pays via the payment gateway.

  5. Michael starts his courses and tracks his progress via his personal dashboard.

* Outcome: Michael gains knowledge in compliance and builds a certification path for his career.

---

Scenario 3: Staff Learner Completing Assigned Course

* Persona: Staff Learner (Inside Organization)

* Context: Funke, an HR staff member, has been assigned NDPR (Data Protection) awareness training by her organization’s admin.

* Steps:

  1. Funke receives a notification that a new course has been assigned.

  2. She logs in from RegTech 365 platform,  
  3. Enters learning then sees the course on her dashboard with a 10-day deadline.

  4. She starts the training, completes microlearning modules, and answers quizzes.

  5. Progress is logged, and her completion is reported back to the organization admin.

* Outcome: Funke completes her training, and her compliance status is updated for audit readiness.

---

Scenario 4: Paid Professional Training with Certification (Future)

* Persona: Independent Learner or Selected Staff

* Context: Tunde, a mid-level IT manager, wants to take a 3-month ISO 27001 Implementer course with certification.

* Steps:

  1. Tunde selects the course and pays via the platform.

  2. The course includes interactive content, quizzes, and a final assessment.

  3. Upon completion, Tunde is issued a digital certificate.

* Outcome: Tunde boosts his professional credentials while the platform logs his training history.

* ### Parent-Facing Compliance Reports

  Decision: Not included in the initial release.

These will be introduced in later phases once the core compliance workflows and audit management modules are stabilized and widely adopted.

  Decision: Not included in the initial release.  
   Reason: Integrating with third-party BI tools (such as Power BI, Tableau, or Qlik) is beneficial for advanced analytics and external reporting. BI integrations will be considered in future phases to enhance enterprise-level analytics capabilities.

  Decision: Not included in the initial release.

**Product Name**: RegTech365 Learning   
 **Product Type**: SaaS Learning Management System (LMS) for Compliance Training

## **1\. Product Vision**

To automate compliance education, reduce reliance on external consultants, and provide organisations with audit-ready, measurable training evidence. The platform empowers both staff and compliance officers with structured, modular, and trackable learning experiences, all seamlessly integrated into the RegTech365 ecosystem.

## **2\. The Proposed Solution**

A digital-first Learning Management System (LMS) for compliance that:

* Serves both organisations (internal training & audit readiness) and individual learners (awareness \+ professional development).

* Provides awareness and professional training paths.

* Automates notifications, progress tracking, and reporting.

* Integrates into the RegTech365 compliance ecosystem (audit, gap assessment, document management).

## **3\. Platform Goals**

* Centralise access to compliance training for organisations & individuals.

* Increase engagement with microlearning and reminders.

* Provide admin-level monitoring and progress reporting.

* Enable organisations to prove compliance with audit-ready records.

* Create scalable revenue via certification paths and professional training.

## **4\. Target Users**

A. Organisations within the RegTech365 ecosystem

* Seamless access into the Learning platform from the Regtech365 platform. Simply clicking on Learning.

* Onboard staff automatically via existing profiles (Name, Email information available from regtech 365 platform).

* Admins assign courses, monitor, nudge, and download reports.

B. Organisations outside the ecosystem

* Self-signup, onboard staff via CSV upload (Name, Email).

* Browse courses, purchase licenses/seats, assign training.

C. Individuals

* Browse and enroll in awareness or professional courses.

* Track progress via dashboard (Coursera-style).

* Pay for certification paths or advanced training via cart.

* For compliance officers, lead auditors, implementers.

* Paid, seat-based access (orgs choose seats & certificates).

* Certificates issued upon completion.

* Mobile app for learners.

* Gamified learning (badges, leaderboards).

* Integration with HR and audit tools.

* Certificate verification portal.

## 11\. Risks & Mitigations

* Risk: Staff ignore emails → *Mitigation:* limit nudges, strong subject lines.

* Risk: Certificate misuse → *Mitigation:* admin control on seat/certificate allocation.

* Risk: Orgs prefer external consultants → *Mitigation:* highlight audit-readiness \+ cost savings.
